Key Research Findings

1. Increased Depression & Anxiety

University of Pennsylvania (2018): Limiting social media to 30 mins/day reduced loneliness and depression by over 30%.

Source: Hunt et al., Journal of Social and Clinical Psychology

This randomized controlled trial showed that reducing social media use significantly improved well-being, particularly for individuals who reported higher levels of depression at baseline.

2. Body Image & Self-Esteem

Body Image Journal (2022): Just 10 minutes of Instagram use increased body dissatisfaction in 80% of young women.

Source: Fardouly et al., Body Image

The study found that exposure to idealized images on Instagram led to immediate negative effects on body image, particularly when participants engaged in upward social comparisons.

3. Sleep Disruption

Sleep Medicine Reviews (2021): Nighttime social media use disrupts deep sleep, worsening anxiety and focus.

Source: Scott et al., Sleep Medicine Reviews

Blue light from screens suppresses melatonin production, while engaging content activates the brain, making it harder to fall asleep and reducing sleep quality.

4. Cyberbullying & Suicide Risk

American Psychological Association (2023): Teens spending >3 hrs/day on social media are 2x as likely to report suicidal thoughts.

Source: APA Health Advisory

The APA's review of multiple studies found a strong correlation between excessive social media use and mental health risks, particularly when combined with cyberbullying experiences.

5. ADHD-Like Symptoms

JAMA Psychiatry (2023): High social media use correlates with impulsivity and attention deficits in teens.

Source: Ra et al., JAMA Psychiatry

This longitudinal study found that teens who frequently checked social media showed increased symptoms of inattention and hyperactivity over time, similar to ADHD symptoms.

Tips for Healthy Social Media Use

  1. Set daily time limits (e.g., 30 mins/day).
  2. Turn off notifications to reduce distractions.
  3. Follow uplifting accounts, mute toxic ones.
  4. Take regular "digital detox" breaks.
